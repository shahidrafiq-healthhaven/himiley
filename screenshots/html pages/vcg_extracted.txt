## H2: Why Choose a Vaginal Contraceptive Gel?
# H3: No Hormones Involved
Phexx works without estrogen or progestin, making it a hormone-free contraceptive option. Because it does not alter hormone levels throughout the body, many women explore it as an alternative to hormonal birth control methods.
# H3: Applied Only When Needed
Unlike daily birth control pills, Phexx is used before intercourse rather than on a continuous schedule. This on-demand approach may appeal to women who prefer more flexibility.
# H3: Prescription-Strength Protection
Phexx is an FDA-approved prescription medication reviewed by licensed healthcare providers. For women seeking evidence-based contraception, clinician oversight may provide additional reassurance compared to over-the-counter options.
# H3: Works at the Site of Action
Phexx works locally in the vagina rather than systemically throughout the body. According to official prescribing information, it helps maintain vaginal pH in a range that reduces sperm motility. Because it works locally, it does not involve systemic hormones.

# H3: Frequently Asked Questions
What is a vaginal contraceptive gel and how does it work?
A vaginal contraceptive gel is a prescription birth control method applied before intercourse. Phexxi works locally in the vagina by maintaining vaginal pH in a range that reduces sperm motility.
A vaginal contraceptive gel is a prescription birth control method applied before intercourse. Phexxi works locally in the vagina by maintaining vaginal pH in a range that reduces sperm motility. Is Phexx the only prescription vaginal contraceptive gel available?
Phexx is currently the primary FDA-approved prescription hormone-free vaginal contraceptive gel available in the United States.
Does Phexx contain hormones?
No. Phexx does not contain estrogen or progestin, making it a hormone-free contraceptive option.
How effective is Phexx as birth control?
According to official prescribing information, Phexx is approximately 86% effective with typical use and approximately 93% effective with perfect use when used as directed.
When do I apply Phexx before sex?
Phexx should be applied vaginally immediately before or up to one hour before each act of intercourse according to prescribing instructions.
Does vaginal contraceptive gel have side effects?
Like any prescription medication, Phexx may cause side effects. Commonly reported effects include vaginal burning, itching, discomfort, urinary tract infections, or yeast infections.
Does Phexx protect against STIs?
No. Phexx does not protect against sexually transmitted infections. Condoms remain important for STI prevention.
Can I get a prescription for vaginal contraceptive gel online?
Yes. Through Miley Health, women can complete a secure online consultation reviewed by a licensed healthcare provider.
What happens during the Miley Health consultation?
Patients complete an online intake form covering medical history, medications, and contraception preferences. A licensed clinician reviews the information and may follow up with additional questions before prescribing treatment if appropriate.
Is an in-person doctor visit required?
In many cases, no in-person visit is necessary. Licensed clinicians can review consultations online and determine whether treatment is clinically appropriate.
Is Phexx covered by insurance?
Coverage may vary depending on the patient’s insurance plan. During the consultation process, patients can review pharmacy and payment options available to them.
Can I use Phexx with condoms?
Yes. Phexx may be used alongside condoms and certain barrier methods. Combining methods may improve pregnancy prevention effectiveness.
Is vaginal contraceptive gel used every day?
No. Phexx is an on-demand contraceptive method used before intercourse rather than daily.
Who may not be a good candidate for Phexx?
Certain medical conditions or recurrent urinary tract infections may affect whether Phexx is appropriate. A licensed healthcare provider reviews each patient’s medical history before prescribing treatment.
Can I stop using Phexx anytime?
Yes. Since Phexx is used only before intercourse and does not contain hormones, women can stop using it whenever they choose.

Important Safety Information
Rare cases (0.36%) of bladder and kidney infections have been reported. If you have a history of urinary tract infections that keep coming back, talk to your  doctor about Phexxi.
Contact your healthcare provider if you are experiencing genitourinary side effects such as: vaginal burning, itching, dysuria, genital discomfort including vulvar or perineal pain, yeast infections, urinary tract infection or bacterial vaginosis.
Avoid Phexxi if you or your sexual partner is allergic to lactic acid, citric acid, potassium bitartrate, or any of the ingredients in Phexxi. Stop using Phexxi if you develop an allergic reaction.
Phexxi does not protect against sexually transmitted infections, including HIV. Avoid using Phexxi with a vaginal ring.
